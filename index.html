Raigad Fort

About Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort is a historic hill fort in Maharashtra, India. It served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under the leadership of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort is known for its strong architecture and strategic location.

The fort is located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, about 170 km from Mumbai. It was originally built in the 15th century but was later captured and rebuilt by Shivaji Maharaj in 1656. He made it his capital and was crowned here in 1674.

Raigad Fort holds great historical importance as it represents the power and vision of the Maratha Empire. It is also the place where Shivaji Maharaj spent his final years and where his samadhi (memorial) is located.

Today, Raigad Fort is a popular tourist destination and a symbol of pride for Maharashtra. Visitors can reach the fort by climbing steps or using a ropeway.

Interesting Facts

  • Raigad Fort is situated about 820 meters above sea level.
  • It has a famous marketplace called “Bazarpeth”.
  • The fort includes the king’s court, queen’s quarters, and watchtowers.
  • Shivaji Maharaj’s coronation took place here in 1674.
